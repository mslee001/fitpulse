Intervention Analysis

Before/after comparison across wellness, body composition, and nutrition

RUN ANALYSIS

This tool compares two time periods — a baseline (before) and an intervention period (after) — to detect changes in your metrics. For example, compare the 4 weeks before a dose increase to the 4 weeks after, to see whether HRV, sleep, weight, or other markers shifted.

Picking dates

  • The before window should end the day before your intervention started
  • The after window should begin on the start date of the intervention
  • Matching window lengths (28 vs. 28 days, etc.) gives the cleanest statistical comparison

Washout days (advanced) — skips the first N days of the after window. Useful when an intervention takes time to take effect, like a medication dose increase where the first week is a transition rather than steady-state.
